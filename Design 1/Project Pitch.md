
Idea 1: Guitar helper device

Working name: ChordSense Display

Core features:
- Real-time chord detection and display
- Beginner-oriented digital tab interface
- Frictionless, standalone use
- Slowdown, looping, and replay chords

Comparable existing products:

- Software-Based Chord & Tab Solutions
	- Chordify: subscription based - converts songs into chord charts
	- Moises: AI based stem separation and chord detection - App-centric. Highly popular software
	- Chord AI - Mobile chord detection - AI-based solution
- These are apps, not practice devices. Don't expand further than tab display for playability tools

Audience: Beginner to intermediate electric and acoustic guitarists, looking for a hardware solution without the need for subscriptions or inaccurate tab-only sites

Use-Case story: As an electric guitar beginner, I am having trouble detecting the chords of songs without the need of a paid subscription-based software that only display tabs. I want to present a much more complete and 

Aesthetic theme: Simple Tabs in a digital display with optionality for reviewing chords, looping, and slowing down. Marshall-style tailor made for guitarists

Technology platform: Digital display powered by FPGA + app for additional functionality

Notes:

- Snare drums make it hard, need to isolate

Script:

Of all the hardships of learning guitar, finding tools that actually help you learn a song you love has to be one of the most persistently annoying. To help beginner to intermediate guitarists, I am proposing Chord Sense Display. Chord Sense Display is a dedicated hardware device that enables frictionless real time chord detection and display, using a beginner friendly digital tab interface. With features such as slowdown, looping, and chord isolation, it goes beyond today’s subscription based software. Existing tools like Chordify and Chord AI focus on converting full songs into static chord charts, while Moises uses AI to separate stems and detect chords through a mobile app. Chord Sense Display instead offers a focused always on experience with a Marshall inspired aesthetic and a minimalist digital tab display that feels like real guitar gear. It is powered by an FPGA that performs real time DSP for low latency chord analysis, with optional machine learning support through a companion app when more complex audio separation is needed. Designed to sit on your amp or pedalboard, you turn it on, play, and learn instantly.
